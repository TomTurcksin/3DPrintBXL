Fused Deposition Printing

Fused Deposition Printers or FDPs are the most typpical printers and are very familliar to hobbyiests. For now they have the best quality/cost ratio. We have the Creality CR10MAx that has been subjected to several upgrades (Octo-pi, flexible print bed etc.). This printer has a print volume of 450mm*450mm*470mm and is one of the largest availeble printers anno 2020 at reasonable cost.

Materials

The materials this printer can support include PLA, PET and ABS. Because the stock can become endless, I onle provide in several options listed hereafter but first some general consderations on the material types:

  • PLA is the preferred printing material. Many fillaments are PLA based materials that take advantage of the good printing properties of PLA and combine it with some other properties for example elastic materials, and fluorecent materials. In addition PLA is an ploymere that can easily be recycled and in principle should desolve in water. After testing this (leaeving PLA for over a month in tap water) I could not see any degradation. So it is more resillient that some advertise.
  • ABS is adviced for cases where the parts are used in a warmer environement +80°C. ABS requires higher print temperatres and harsher on the printed than PLA.

If a print is required in a different material, the client is welcome to order a spool, the leftover material belongs to the client. No material costs will be charged but [€] for installing the client spool will be charged. Please contact me before purchasing any material to ensure compatability.
